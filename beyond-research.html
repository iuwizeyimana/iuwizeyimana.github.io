Beyond Research

A small corner of the website for the things that keep me grounded, active, and playful outside the lab.

Outside research, I am happiest outdoors. I keep myself active through several sports, and I have found that movement gives me a kind of clarity that is hard to find anywhere else.

My favourite sport to participate in, although some may argue about the label, is running. Running has given me much more than physical well-being: it has taught me mental fortitude, humility, patience, and a kinder relationship with progress. I grew up around running. My father was a runner, and on some weekends he would bring us along for parts of his runs. Long-distance running, however, became a serious part of my life only after I started my PhD.

Throughout graduate school, running has helped me make sense of the rollercoaster that comes with research: uncertainty, excitement, frustration, breakthroughs, and everything in between. In May 2024, I ran my first half marathon, and two years later, in May 2026, I completed the full distance.

I often describe running as the physical push that mirrors the mental push required in graduate school.

Beyond running, I enjoy ultimate frisbee, tennis, swimming, and biking. Last year, with encouragement from my wonderfully adventurous running friend Neha, I tried a triathlon for the first time. It was a really fun sprint triathlon which got me excited to try longer distances of the sport (eventually; one thing as a time).

This summer, I have a 100 km bikepacking event coming up. I am looking forward to writing a post about that journey and documenting what it teaches me along the way.

More than anything, staying active reminds me to be playful with my body and gentle with my mind. That balance has been crucial while navigating graduate school. Fun is in the sweat!
